About Me

Meredith is a PhD candidate in auditory neuroscience at Purdue University. She works in the Central Auditory Processing (CAP) Lab under the advisement of Edward Bartlett, PhD. Her current research aims to identify biomarkers of peripheral and central auditory system damage distinct to different types of hearing loss to better optimize treatment for each patient's individual needs. It focuses on analyzing electrophysiological responses to traditional and novel diagnostic stimuli to determine characteristics most sensitive to features of damage unique to various hearing loss pathologies. Beyond her PhD work, she is interested in the effects of metabolic disorders and hormone imbalance on neural and auditory systems. When not in the lab, Meredith can be found swing dancing or learning a new recipe.
